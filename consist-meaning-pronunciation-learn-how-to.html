In English, CONSIST, as a VERB, means to be made up of (with ‘of’), to be comprised (with ‘in’).

As a NOUN, it means the railway stock excluding the locomotive engine. It also has other meanings as revealed in this video.

Watch the video to know how to pronounce this English word, how to use it, and other elements related to CONSIST.

CONSIST is not a “pictorial word”. Although this word has its origins in Latin – con sistere, it’s difficult to draw a picture. You have to use it to remember it.

You will need to link it with the picture till you get it automatically. You can do so, only by using it regularly. Some words, especially those that have been introduced as they are from other languages, cannot be easily visualised.
