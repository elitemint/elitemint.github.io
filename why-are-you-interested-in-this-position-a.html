WHY IS THE INTERVIEWER ASKING YOU THE QUESTION, WHY ARE YOU INTERESTED IN THIS POSITION?
Well, the majority of interview candidates just want a job; they want a paycheck, and they are not too bothered who it is with. But the problem with those types of candidates is they don’t end up staying in the job for too long, which is frustrating for the employer.

So, the interviewer is asking you the question, why are you interested in this position, to assess your motivations, and whether or not you have researched the role, and their company.

3 THINGS YOU SHOULD INCLUDE IN YOUR ANSWER TO THE INTERVIEW QUESTION, WHY ARE YOU INTERESTED IN THIS POSITION?
#1. Mention the fact that you are seeking a position that enables you to grow and develop both personally and professionally.

#2. Mention that you feel this position is one that you can ADD VALUE in due to the fact your skills, qualities and experience are a match for the job description.

#3. Mention that, having researched their company, you are attracted to their values, their previous track record of success, and their ambitious plans for the future.

LET’S NOW TAKE A LOOK AT AN EXAMPLE ANSWER TO THE INTERVIEW QUESTION, WHY ARE YOU INTERESTED IN THIS POSITION?
“I am interested in this position for several reasons. The first reason is because I am passionate about this type of work; it is something I very much enjoy doing, and the skills and qualities I possess will mean I can add value to your company in this role.

I am also interested in this position because having researched your company, I am attracted to your values, your track record of achievement, and the fact that you clearly have ambitious plans for the future.

This position, if I am successful, is one that I can see myself working in for many years to come whilst growing both personally and professionally.”
