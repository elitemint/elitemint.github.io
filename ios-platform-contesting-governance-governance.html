The research platform Contesting Governance of Utrecht University unites researchers who apply critical perspectives on how power is constituted across the world, how it operates, and how it is contested. We study questions such as:

How is state violence contested by civil society? How do citizens respond when they feel unprotected, abandoned or attacked by the state? How do people voice concerns and make claims in the streets, or in the courts? How do people negotiate their everyday needs and livelihoods when (private) armed actors control their material and social spaces, and decide their fate? When the state no longer governs, how do rebels rule?

Within the platform Contesting Governance of Utrecht Universityâ€™s strategic theme Institutions for Open Societies, we therefore combine expertise from several disciplines to gain comprehensive insights. Researchers from different fields work together with societal partners to jointly investigate what is needed to contribute to open and resilient societies.

Hoe bestrijden maatschappelijke organisaties en protestbewegingen geweld van staten? Hoe reageren burgers als ze zich niet beschermd voelen, in de steek gelaten of zelfs aangevallen doro de overheid? Hoe onderhandelen burgers over wat ze in hun dagelijks leven nodig hebben in gebieden waar gewapende groepen heersen? En als er geen staat meer is, hoe regeren rebellen dan?
