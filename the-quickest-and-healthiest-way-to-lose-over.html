Summary:
- Losing weight requires you to be 100% committed. You can’t commit to your weight loss goals for a week then take a week off, especially if your weight loss goal is a large number. You need to realize that on average, a healthy weight loss ranges from 0.5lbs to 3lbs a week.
- Losing that amount of weight a week is hard to sustain and often results in a relapse. So having the proper mindset will help you realize that this is not going to be a quick fix and will take some time.
- As I mentioned earlier, losing weight takes a lot of time and dedication. During this time, vacations, outings and birthdays will come up, so you will have to make changes and sacrifices at certain times. It’s not going to be easy, but if you’re serious about your goals and results, you can do it. It might also help to communicate with your friends and family, they will support you and may even make changes to certain events to fit your goals.
- Losing upward to 50 pounds of weight means drastically changing your habits, and this won’t happen overnight. Shaking habits is one of the hardest things to do in life.
- One thing you need to remember is that the occasional slip up isn’t the worst thing in the world. The key is to get back on track as soon as possible. However, if you find that one slip up leads to another, you will need to search a little deeper to find out what the major issue is.
- The biggest mistake people make when trying to lose weight is to do it because they hate their body. What makes people special is not how their body looks, but how they are inside. If you love yourself, then you’ll know that your body will appreciate being healthy. Go and get in shape.
