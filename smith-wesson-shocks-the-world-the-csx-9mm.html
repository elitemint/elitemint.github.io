A totally new design, Smith and Wesson brings us the surprising CSX. Maybe you can think of it as a combination of Sig P938 and a Shield Plus. Continuing an industry trend, this pistol has a few more ounces than the striker fired concealed carry kings like the Sig P365 and S.A. Hellcat but it's still light at 20 oz. That and a single action only (SAO) trigger will make the CSX easier to hit with. It's an interesting and new concealed carry pistol formula. It does have some quirks and these are addressed in this feature length Nutnfancy gun review. Snow testing was accomplished. Accuracy, reliability, firepower, sights, finishing, ergos, and shootability are shown/discussed. A rare appearance of Mrs Nutnfancy helping with the shooting is part of this timely, feature length TNP GRV!
