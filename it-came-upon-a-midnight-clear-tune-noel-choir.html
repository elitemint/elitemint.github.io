The Choir of Winchester Cathedral and the Waynflete Singers, under the direction of David Hill, and accompanied by the Resounding Brass, perform the 'Noel' setting of the popular Christmas carol 'It Came Upon a Midnight Clear'. This setting of the carol takes is text from a poem by Edmund Spears and its melody from a tune adapted by Arthur Sullivan.



Sullivan's tune, often known as 'Noel', is the more commonly heard setting of 'It Came Upon a Midnight Clear' in Britain but is in fact a newer musical setting of the text than is Richard Storrs Willis' melody 'Carol'. 'Carol' is more recognisable in the United States.
