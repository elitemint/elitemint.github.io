--- title: "Mayo Clinic Minute: Keeping those who are medically vulnerable safe from COVID 19" image: "https:\/\/i.ytimg.com\/vi\/d1e4fz_kRNk\/hqdefault.jpg" vid_id: "d1e4fz_kRNk" categories: "Science-Technology" tags: ["Mayo Clinic","Health Care (Issue)","Healthcare Science (Field Of Study)"] date: "2021-11-10T02:46:03+03:00" vid_date: "2021-11-08T12:16:19Z" duration: "PT1M30S" viewcount: "822" likeCount: "24" dislikeCount: "6" channel: "Mayo Clinic" --- {% raw %}If you or a loved one are immunocompromised, and therefore at a higher risk of becoming severely ill from COVID-19, it's more important than ever to plan ahead for upcoming holiday gatherings. Among those at heightened risk are cancer and transplant patients who are taking immunosuppression medication, in addition to patients with advanced and untreated HIV/AIDS.

In this Mayo Clinic Minute, Dr. Raymund Razonable, a Mayo Clinic infectious diseases physician who works with transplant patients, will explain the steps patients and their loved ones can take to reduce their risk of getting infected with COVID-19 during the holiday season.
___________________
For the safety of its patients, staff and visitors, Mayo Clinic has strict masking policies in place. Anyone shown without a mask was recorded prior to COVID-19 or recorded in an area not designated for patient care, where social distancing and other safety protocols were followed.
