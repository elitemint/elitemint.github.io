The Mumbai–Nagpur Expressway or Nagpur–Mumbai Super Communication Expressway (officially known as Hindu Hrudaysamarat Balasaheb Thackeray Maharashtra Samruddhi Mahamarg) is an under-construction 701 km long and 6-lane wide (expandable to 8-lane) access-controlled expressway in Maharashtra, India. It will be amongst the country's longest Greenfield road project, connecting the two capitals of the state i.e., Mumbai and Nagpur. The project is led by the state infrastructure arm Maharashtra State Road Development Corporation (MSRDC) and is designed under the Engineering, Procurement, and Construction (EPC) model.
