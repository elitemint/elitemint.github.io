Houses like toys! Awful severe winds hit Istanbul, Turkey

A powerful storm pounded Istanbul and other parts of Turkey, causing havoc in the city of 15 million people.

Gusty winds reached a speed of 130 kilometers per hour.

The winds blew away 33 roofs and uprooted more than 200 trees. Storm knocked down 52 traffic lights and road signs and damaged more than 12 cars. The gusts also knocked down a clock tower.

The Bosporus strait was closed to maritime traffic and ferry services were canceled. Fishing boats sank in the Pendik district. At least six Turkish Airlines flights were diverted to the cities of Ankara and Izmir. The strong winds also forced authorities to cancel ferry services between the coastal resorts of Bodrum and Datca.

The State Meteorological Service issued warnings against adverse weather conditions for 52 provinces.
