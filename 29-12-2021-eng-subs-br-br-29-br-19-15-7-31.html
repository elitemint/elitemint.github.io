Greek-Turkish relations.
GREECE. TURKEY. CYPRUS.
Another big drop in the exchange rate of the Turkish lira against the US dollar, we have today, Wednesday, December 29, which seems to be collapsing again, and even "without brakes", proving that the plan of Turkish President Tayyip Erdogan, to compensate depositors, who maintain accounts in Turkish lira and to give them the difference, if the exchange rate, is higher than the interest rate they have in the bank, does not have the trust of investors and Turkish citizens.
So more specifically, at 19:15 Greek time, we can see the Turkish lira losing 7.31% compared to yesterday and the exchange rate of one dollar being at 12.65 Turkish lira, breaking the barrier of 12 again, and clearly showing an upward trend.
At this point, let's say that there have been "awkward" moves by the Turkish government, such as lawsuits against Turkish economic analysts, which has done more harm than good, as everyone knows that markets are based primarily on trust.
And what trust can you have when the principles of the Turkish capital market are silenced financial analysts, so that they do not express their views.
This even shows the panic that prevails in the Turkish financial staff, as there is a lot of rumor that Turkey's foreign exchange reserves are now exhausted, something that will be seen from whether the Turkish central bank intervenes again, selling dollars and buying lira.
